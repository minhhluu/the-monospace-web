About me (in 3rd person)

Minh Luu is a Vietnamese engineer and contemplative thinker who moves between hardware design and inner reflection.

His work spans FPGA systems, embedded design, and experimental physics — from modeling quantum effects to building real-time imaging systems on the Zynq-7000 platform.

He views engineering as a form of mindfulness - a way to sense rhythm, structure, and meaning through precision. Minh calls this approach "meditative contemplation in engineering".

Beyond circuits, he explores philosophy and consciousness, drawing from Buddhist insight and Kantian thought to question how understanding arises in both humans and machines.

Minh values depth in conversation and clarity in design. When not building or reflecting, he enjoys moments of stillness — letting ideas settle, the way signals fade into silence.
